📖 Similar Triangles — Guided Lesson
Step 1 of 7
What Are Similar Triangles?
Two triangles are similar when they have the same shape but are not necessarily the same size.

For two triangles to be similar:
• All corresponding angles must be equal
• All corresponding sides must be proportional
📐 We write similarity using the ~ symbol. Example: △ABC ~ △DEF means triangle ABC is similar to triangle DEF.
Step 2 of 7
Setting Up Triangle 1
Look at the blue panel on the right labeled "Triangle 1 — Reference."

• Enter values for Angle A and Angle B
• Angle C calculates automatically because all angles in a triangle must sum to 180°
• Adjust Scale to change the size of Triangle 1
🔑 Key Fact: The three interior angles of any triangle always sum to 180°. This is called the Triangle Angle Sum Theorem.

Step 4 of 7
The AA Similarity Postulate
The Angle-Angle (AA) Similarity Postulate states:

If two angles of one triangle are congruent to two angles of another triangle, then the two triangles are similar.

You only need to verify two pairs of angles — the third pair will automatically be equal since all angles must sum to 180°.
🎯 Try it: Drag Triangle 2 until two of its angles match Triangle 1. The postulate badge should display AA Similarity!
Step 5 of 7
Proportional Side Ratios
When two triangles are similar, their corresponding sides are always in the same ratio. Look at the Side Ratios panel.

• The ratios AB/DE, BC/EF, and CA/FD should all be equal
• This common ratio is called the scale factor
• The scale factor tells you how many times larger or smaller one triangle is
📏 Try resizing Triangle 2 by dragging its vertices outward. Notice the individual side lengths change, but the ratios between corresponding sides stay equal!
Step 6 of 7
Similarity Transformations
Similarity is preserved through the following transformations — the triangles remain similar even after:

• Dilation — resizing changes the size but not the shape
• Rotation — spinning the triangle any number of degrees
• Reflection — flipping the triangle horizontally or vertically
🔄 Try the Transformations panel! Rotate and dilate Triangle 2 using the sliders. Flip it using Reflect H or V. Notice the Similarity Status stays ✅ Similar throughout!
Step 7 of 7
SSS and SAS Similarity
There are two additional similarity postulates:

• SSS Similarity: If all three pairs of corresponding sides are proportional, the triangles are similar.

• SAS Similarity: If two pairs of corresponding sides are proportional AND the included angles between those sides are congruent, the triangles are similar.
🏆 You now know all three similarity postulates: AA, SSS, and SAS. The postulate badge in the Similarity Status panel identifies which one applies as you explore!
