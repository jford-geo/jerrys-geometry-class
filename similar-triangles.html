📖 Similar Triangles — Guided Lesson
Step 1 of 7
What Are Similar Triangles?
Two triangles are similar when they have the same shape but not necessarily the same size.

For two triangles to be similar, ALL of the following must be true:
• All three pairs of corresponding angles must be congruent
• All three pairs of corresponding sides must be proportional
📐 We write similarity using the ~ symbol. Example: △ABC ~ △DEF. The ORDER of letters matters — it identifies which angles and sides correspond to each other.

🔑 Triangle Angle Sum Theorem: The three interior angles of ANY triangle always sum to exactly 180°.

Step 4 of 7
The AA Similarity Postulate
The Angle-Angle (AA) Similarity Postulate states:

If two angles of one triangle are congruent to two angles of another triangle, then the triangles are similar.

Why does only two angles suffice? Because if two pairs match, the third MUST also match since all angles sum to 180°!
🎯 Try it: Drag Triangle 2 until angle D ≈ angle A AND angle E ≈ angle B. Notice angle F automatically equals angle C, AND all three side ratios become equal simultaneously!
Step 5 of 7
Proportional Side Ratios
When triangles are truly similar, look at the Side Ratios panel:

• AB/DE, BC/EF, and CA/FD will ALL be equal
• This common ratio is the scale factor
• If ANY ratio differs, the triangles are NOT similar
📏 Key insight: Matching angles and proportional sides ALWAYS occur together in similar triangles. You cannot have one without the other!
Step 6 of 7
Similarity Transformations
Similarity is preserved through these transformations:

• Dilation — resizing changes size but NOT shape
• Rotation — spinning any number of degrees
• Reflection — flipping horizontally or vertically
🔄 Try it: First make the triangles similar by dragging. Then use the Transformations panel to rotate, dilate, and reflect Triangle 2. The ✅ Similar status remains throughout!
Step 7 of 7
SSS and SAS Similarity
Two more ways to prove similarity:

• SSS Similarity: All three pairs of corresponding sides are proportional

• SAS Similarity: Two pairs of corresponding sides are proportional AND the included angle between those sides is congruent
🏆 You now know all three similarity postulates: AA, SSS, and SAS. The postulate badge updates automatically to show which one applies!
